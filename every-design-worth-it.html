How to reach your users and get feedback about your design

Talking to customers is important to every business,but if you don’t reserve a timeslot in your weekly routine,it won’t happen.

1. Start your research from the inside

I was scheduling a usability test when a friend from the Support Team just came over and said: “Did you see the latest report about user doubts? “Yes. I did.” – I said. “So why are you trying to get insights when they’re popping out at us everyday?” Bam! She was right. I double checked the report and it was clear where I should focus on my research: The point is: I was trying to plan a test from zero when there were lacks already known in the products that I could use as a starting point for my research. It would save me a lot of time if I had checked this out before! It doesn’t mean as a designer I shouldn’t take the time and interact with my customers by myself… but you have to keep in mind that there are people in other teams who already do that. Everyday. So go to talk to them, schedule a 15min weekly meeting and let them share what’s coming up regularly from users. Gather the data. And then start planning your research.
