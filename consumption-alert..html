SmartServe Ordering System

Redefining ordering restaurants experience using a mobile App/website.

Overview

About the project

In this project, I present a smartphone application which solves some of the major problems faced by customers and restaurant ordering management by integrating existing technologies and services to help people save time, money,comment on the food quality cooked by the Chef in a certain restaurant and create a memorable shopping experience whilst cutting number of wiater(ss) in a restaurant and helping a restaurants to cut cut costs/make 200% profits.

Client: Restaurants

Duration: 2.6 Months

Tools Used: Adobe Photoshop, JustInMind,Balsamiq prototyping,AdobeXd,Paper&Pen

My Role: UX / UI Designer (Please note the rest of the Project I continued with it after I left the company as it was still on User-Flow stage stage)

The Challenge

How can I improve in-store restaurants ordering experience?

Although we have all the technology at our disposal, the restaurants experience has not much advanced with the times. A lot of shoppers still face inconveniences in finding ordering online/ using apps when ordering, deciding which food to buy, getting discounts & spending time in checkout lines only to find what you are ordering is not available or out of stock.

Research

Identifying the problems

The main goal of the research was to understand the goals, motivations and pain points of the user so that I could design a better solution for them.

Interviews

I conducted semi-structured interviews with 10 regular customers for different restaurants experience to understand the problem space and their overall experience of ordering food from restaurants outlets/stores. This helped us to explore different problems & go deep down into few major issues that they currently face.

Market Research

I studied some research. Some key results of this study showed that though restaurants outlets/stores are still using the old source for food ordering among all age groups, customers are using digital tools to order food more frequently.

User Persona

Understanding the user

Based on our research data I created two different personas that represent the problems faced by our respondents.

Journey Mapping

Analyzing behaviour & experiences

I created journey maps to get an in-depth knowledge about our users, their tasks, feelings, experiences and their mood at different points of time.

Research insights

Framing the problem

The major problems and needs are listed as follows:

  1. Users end up spending too much time in the restaurants outlets/store especially in things such as standing in checkout lines and finding what to order.
  2. Users want to see the reviews of food prepared by the Chef and compare prices.
  3. Users want to save money by finding food that are discounted or on special.
  4. Users want to know how much they are spending and compare food quality with price.
Brainstorming

Sketching ideas & use cases

After getting a good understanding of the problem space and the opportunities, I started brainstorming and sketching out different ideas/concepts to help our users.

Integrating SmartServe

When researching about existing solutions, I came across UberEats,MrD Foods and much more. It is is a restaurant and retail concept that utilizes several technologies, including automated ordering,checkout and payment steps. Basically, you could simply order online using the App, and get your food delivered or collect it to the preferred pick-up points without the hassle of stading in checkout lines and paying to the cashier or within the App. I realized the solution was incredible, and wanted to expand upon it and not re-invent the wheel given that users usually face many other issues while ordering at the restaurants outlets/store.

Selected features

Defining the functionalities

I finally decided the main features of our proposed solution 'SmartServe' which integrates UberEats,Instead of Ordering and get your food delivered either it's fully cooked or not to add a feature to send a review food quality to the Chef,or restaurant quality of service and can order using the App within the restaurant without the interference of a waiter(ss)

Food on Special

Users will be able to check the on prices of food available in certain restaurants and place the order if wants to and specify table/address.

Locating Your order/table

The solution will facilitate users order faster without have to wait long,find what they are looking for.

Food or Chef Reviews

Facilitate and help users to make decisions while ordering by connecting with previous food/Chef reviews from other users.

Transparent Prices

SmartServe customers will get the lowest price food by Default by doing so I know most people like low price food.

PRODUCT TESTING

Evaluations & walkthroughs

I conducted informal and formal cognitive walkthroughs. Informal testing was done in a controlled environment using paper prototypes & the formal testing was done with the AdobeXd prototype at a restaurant.

Informal Walkthroughs

I conducted informal walkthroughs twice with our prototypes. The evaluators were some students at Joburg Center For Software Engineering along with Walter Sisulu University (Mthatha Campus) and were frequent online/in person restaurants customers. I printed out the paper prototypes and tested. Both the rounds provided us with some valuable feedbacks for further exploration and design.

Formal Walkthroughs

I recruited three participants at a local restaurant, to test out high fidelity prototype. I asked each of them complete three tasks that utilizes primary features of the app and to think aloud the experience, thoughts or ideas regarding the prototype.

From this testing, I hoped to answer the following questions:

  1. Are the features of application useful when the users are ordering to different restaurants?
  2. Is this application easy to use? (Will the user know how to use this application?)
  3. Are there any changes to be made to improve this application?

Key Findings from Testing

Overall, the participants were very positive toward our prototype. Most of them did not have issues in completing tasks and appreciated the simplicity of design.One participant wanted to have a feature that allows to check other restaurants nearby that offers same item prices, so that the participant can decide whether they want to make a trip to that restaurant to save money. Furthermore, one participant pointed that the users may take someone's order when other person went to the lavetry tables in a restaurant when they are using this service.

Conclusion

Reflection & next steps

From this project, I had a valuable experience of testing with actual users in the actual environment this application is designed to be used. Although it was quite anxious at first to ask someone I do not know for a participation, at the end of the day, I learned that people were quite willing to add their insights about current technologies used and the improvements of the current technologies to latest ones regarding the iterative process, I realized that the design can look very different depending on the medium it is being presented. When I used the paper prototype, none of the participants had any problem with font size, because the design was printed on A4 size papers. But when I moved the design to a cell phone screen, the participants started to point issues with font size.

Limitations

The biggest limitation of our project would be that the AR technology was not implemented in our prototype. Therefore, I had to ask the participants to act as if the prototype had the AR feature. If our prototype could have incorporated the technology, it would have led to more discoveries on potential problems our prototype may have. Another limitation is that our participants for testing were mostly young adults. I do have results from elders, who may be less familiar with AR technology or online ordering system than the youth participants I tested the prototype with.

Design wishes / future work:

  1. Sorting searching and content on search
  2. Making the app available on microsoft,the App to be used by small,medium and large restaurants and a way to intergrate the App to accomodate retail shopping experience too
  3. Restaurants to renovate their sitting or table structure as users will order whilst sitted in a certain table and food be delivered to that table and to cut out 85% of their current waiter(ss) employees
  4. Auto suggesting products based on ordering history
  5. Having delivery of food by including drones or other means of transportation
