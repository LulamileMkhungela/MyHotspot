SmartServe Ordering System

Redefining ordering restaurants experience using a mobile App/website.

Overview

About the project

In this project, we present a smartphone application which solves some of the major problems faced by customers by integrating existing technologies and services to help people save time, money,comment on the food quality cooked by the Chef in a certain restaurant and create a memorable shopping experience.

Timeline: 2 Months

My Role: Interaction Design, Prototyping & Evaluation

The Challenge

How can we improve in-store restaurants ordering experience?

Although we have all the technology at our disposal, the restaurants experience has not much advanced with the times. A lot of shoppers still face inconveniences in finding ordering online/ using apps when ordering, deciding which food to buy, getting discounts & spending time in checkout lines only to find what you are ordering is not available or out of stock.

Research

Identifying the problems

The main goal of the research was to understand the goals, motivations and pain points of the user so that we could design a better solution for them.

Interviews

We conducted semi-structured interviews with 10 regular customers for different restaurants experience to understand the problem space and their overall experience of ordering food from restaurants outlets/stores. This helped us to explore different problems & go deep down into few major issues that they currently face.

Market Research

We studied some research. Some key results of this study showed that though restaurants outlets/stores are still using the old source for food ordering among all age groups, customers are using digital tools to order food more frequently.

Research insights

Framing the problem

The major problems and needs are listed as follows:

  1. Users end up spending too much time in the restaurants outlets/store especially in things such as standing in checkout lines and finding what to order.
  2. Users want to see the reviews of food prepared by the Chef and compare prices.
  3. Users want to save money by finding food that are discounted or on special.
  4. Users want to know how much they are spending and compare food quality with price.
Brainstorming

Sketching ideas & use cases

After getting a good understanding of the problem space and the opportunities, we started brainstorming and sketching out different ideas/concepts to help our users.

Integrating SmartServe

When researching about existing solutions, we came across UberEats,MrD Foods and much more. It is is a restaurant and retail concept that utilizes several technologies, including automated ordering,checkout and payment steps. Basically, you could simply order online using the App, and get your food delivered or collect it to the preferred pick-up points without the hassle of stading in checkout lines and paying to the cashier or within the App. We realized the solution was incredible, and wanted to expand upon it and not re-invent the wheel given that users usually face many other issues while ordering at the restaurants outlets/store.

Selected features

Defining the functionalities

We finally decided the main features of our proposed solution 'SmartServe' which integrates UberEats,Instead of Ordering and get your food delivered either it's fully cooked or not to add a feature to send a review food quality to the Chef,or restaurant quality of service and can order using the App within the restaurant without the interference of a waiter(ss)

Food on Special

Users will be able to check the on prices of food available in certain restaurants and place the order if wants to and specify table/address.

Locating Your order/table

The solution will facilitate users order faster without have to wait long,find what they are looking for.

Food or Chef Reviews

Facilitate and help users to make decisions while ordering by connecting with previous food/Chef reviews from other users.

Transparent Prices

SmartServe customers will get the lowest price food by Default by doing so we know most people like low price food.

The Solution

Design decisions

For the first round of prototype, we used Balsamiq to create image files, printed them out in A4 size and used as paper prototype. After the feedback from the users we did second round of prototyping with the changes suggested and some layout changes.

Welcome page(image)

Home screen(image)

When opening the app, the first screen that comes up has a welcome message. We adopted this feature from current UX/UI trends for restaurants experience, which starts with a user by browsing items in that restaurant that a user is at,scan a QR code at the entrance of the restaurant.

Then, after a user scans a QR code, the user can use the main menu consisting of the four primary functions: showing items on special, locating an order to certain table, even scanning an item on restaurant pamphlet to show detailed information, and checking items in cart.

The Prototype
PRODUCT TESTING

Evaluations & walkthroughs

We conducted informal and formal cognitive walkthroughs. Informal testing was done in a controlled environment using paper prototypes & the formal testing was done with the AdobeXd prototype at a restaurant.

Informal Walkthroughs

We conducted informal walkthroughs twice with our prototypes. The evaluators were my Mentor Anaurd Kim Mukenge and his students at Joburg Center For Software Engineering and were frequent customers. I printed out the paper prototypes and tested. Both the rounds provided us with some valuable feedbacks for further exploration and design.

Formal Walkthroughs

We recruited three participants at a local restaurant, to test out high fidelity prototype. We asked each of them complete three tasks that utilizes primary features of the app and to think aloud the experience, thoughts or ideas regarding the prototype.

From this testing, we hoped to answer the following questions:

  1. Are the features of application useful when the users are ordering to different restaurants?
  2. Is this application easy to use? (Will the user know how to use this application?)
  3. Are there any changes to be made to improve this application?

Key Findings from Testing

Overall, the participants were very positive toward our prototype. Most of them did not have issues in completing tasks and appreciated the simplicity of design. When checking prices, one participant wanted to check the location of the restaurants that offers the item in the lower price, so that the participant can decide whether they want to make a trip to that restaurant to save money. Furthermore, one participant pointed that the users may take someone's order when other person went to the lavetry tables in a restaurant when they are using this service.

Conclusion

Reflection & next steps

From this project, I had a valuable experience of testing with actual users in the actual environment this application is designed to be used. Although it was quite anxious at first to ask someone we do not know for a participation, at the end of the day, I learned that people were quite willing to add their insights about current technologies used and the improvements of the current technologies to latest ones regarding the iterative process, I realized that the design can look very different depending on the medium it is being presented. When we used the paper prototype, none of the participants had any problem with font size, because the design was printed on A4 size papers. But when we moved the design to a cell phone screen, the participants started to point issues with font size.

Limitations

The biggest limitation of our project would be that the AR technology was not implemented in our prototype. Therefore, we had to ask the participants to act as if the prototype had the AR feature. If our prototype could have incorporated the technology, it would have led to more discoveries on potential problems our prototype may have. Another limitation is that our participants for testing were mostly young adults. We do have results from elders, who may be less familiar with AR technology or online ordering system than the youth participants we tested the prototype with.

Design wishes / future work:

  1. Sorting searching and content on search
  2. Making the app available on microsoft,the App to be used by small,medium and large restaurants and a way to intergrate the App to accomodate retail shopping experience too
  3. Restaurants to renovate their sitting or table structure as users will order whilst sitted in a certain table and food be delivered to that table and to cut out 85% of their current waiter(ss) employees
  4. Auto suggesting products based on ordering history
  5. Having delivery of food by including drones or other means of transportation
